### 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- **Reversal shorts print bigger when the stock is more extended.** The top 5 predictors of reversal P&L are all extension metrics — day-of range expansion, distance from 9EMA, 3-day momentum, distance from 10MA, and 15-day momentum. All have Spearman correlations above 0.50 (very strong). The more parabolic the setup, the bigger the payoff.

- **Bounce longs work best on deeply beaten-down names.** The #1 predictor of bounce P&L is pct_change_3 (ρ = -0.68) — the steeper the 3-day selloff, the bigger the bounce. Upper Bollinger band distance (ρ = -0.50) and 15-day momentum (ρ = -0.42) confirm: deeper capitulation = fatter bounce.

- **Bounces love a green SPY day.** When SPY closes green on the bounce day, the win rate is 91% with +11.4% avg P&L (N=67). When SPY is flat/neutral, expectancy flips negative at -4.0% (N=13). Borderline significant (p=0.052) but the effect is large and the sample is decent.

- **Broader market downtrend is the ideal bounce backdrop.** Bounces taken during SPY downtrends average +11.3% (89% WR, N=65) vs +0.95% in SPY uptrends (N=7). The setup needs a fearful market to create capitulation — but the actual entry day should be a relief rally.

- **Premarket volume is a reversal quality signal.** For reversals, higher premarket volume correlates with better P&L (ρ=0.48), while a high 5min-to-premarket volume ratio hurts (ρ=-0.48). Translation: you want the hype concentrated in premarket, not a fresh flood at the open.

---

### 2. KEY TAKEAWAYS

**For Reversals:**
The data is screaming one thing: *extension is king*. The top 6 Spearman correlations are all measuring the same underlying concept — how stretched the stock is from normal. day_of_range_pct (ρ=0.58), pct_from_9ema (ρ=0.56), pct_change_3 (ρ=0.55), pct_from_10mav (ρ=0.53), pct_change_15 (ρ=0.51), and percent_of_premarket_vol (ρ=0.48) all point to: the more extreme the move, the harder the reversal snaps. With 29 of 32 features significant, this isn't noise — the entire feature set is correlated with the extension thesis.

**For Bounces:**
The mirror image holds — depth of selloff is the #1 driver. pct_change_3 at ρ=-0.68 is an exceptionally strong correlation for market data. Notably, atr_pct (ρ=0.38, higher=better) suggests higher-volatility names bounce harder — which makes sense because they have more mean-reversion fuel. The gap_pct finding (ρ=-0.32, lower=better) confirms: bigger gap downs set up bigger bounces.

**Regime Insight (Bounce):**
The ideal bounce setup is: broad market in a downtrend (fear creates the capitulation), but the specific day you enter, SPY is green (the relief rally lifts all boats). This isn't contradictory — it's the classic "buy the dip in a pullback, not in a freefall" pattern. The N=13 neutral-SPY bucket with -4% avg P&L is the danger zone: no broad fear to create the setup, no green day to lift the bounce.

---

### 3. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS

**Reversal Strategy:**
1. **Rank setups by extension composite.** Create a simple scoring system weighting pct_from_9ema, day_of_range_pct, and pct_change_3. Size up on the most extended setups — they have materially higher expected payoff.
2. **Use premarket volume as a quality filter.** High premarket volume + low 5min/PM ratio = the move is exhausting itself premarket rather than accelerating at open. Fade these more aggressively.
3. **Don't waste bullets on marginal extension.** If pct_from_9ema is barely positive, the expected value of the short drops substantially. Consider a minimum threshold (needs quantification in next session).

**Bounce Strategy:**
1. **Gate on selloff depth.** pct_change_3 is so dominant (ρ=-0.68) that a simple filter — only take bounces where the 3-day decline exceeds some threshold — would likely improve expectancy meaningfully. Derive the optimal cutoff.
2. **Add a SPY-green confirmation rule.** With 91% WR and +11.4% avg on green SPY days vs negative expectancy on flat SPY days, consider waiting for SPY to confirm green before entering. The cost is occasionally missing the open, but the hit rate improvement is substantial.
3. **Favor high-ATR names.** atr_pct correlating positively with P&L means volatile names bounce harder. When multiple setups fire simultaneously, prioritize the higher-ATR ticker.

### 4. NEXT SESSION IDEAS

1. **Threshold optimization for reversal extension filters.** We know pct_from_9ema and day_of_range_pct predict P&L — now we need to find the optimal cutoffs. Run a stratification experiment splitting reversal trades into terciles/quartiles by pct_from_9ema and day_of_range_pct, measuring expectancy in each bucket. This directly converts the correlation finding into a tradeable filter. Break out by cap size since thresholds likely differ (Micro vs Large).

2. **Bounce selloff-depth threshold by cap size.** pct_change_3 is the single strongest predictor across either strategy. Stratify bounce trades into "shallow selloff" vs "deep selloff" buckets (e.g., above/below median pct_change_3) and compare expectancy. Do this per cap tier — a 10% selloff means something different for an ETF vs a Micro cap. This gives you a concrete "minimum selloff" gate per cap.

3. **SPY regime interaction with reversal P&L.** We only ran regime analysis on bounces. Reversals may also have regime sensitivity — parabolic shorts might work differently in bull vs bear markets, or when SPY is red (risk-off unwinds momentum). Running this fills a gap and could reveal a timing edge for the short side.
