### 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- **Reversal shorts print bigger when the stock is more extended.** The single best predictor of reversal P&L is day-of range expansion (ρ=0.58), followed by distance from 9EMA (ρ=0.56) and 3-day momentum (ρ=0.55). These are all measuring the same thing: how parabolic the move is. The more stretched, the harder the snap-back. This isn't surprising, but it's now quantified — and it means filtering OUT low-extension setups should meaningfully lift expectancy.

- **Bounce longs print bigger when the selloff is deeper.** The #1 predictor of bounce P&L is 3-day price change (ρ=-0.68 — that's a strong correlation). Deeper selloffs = bigger bounces. Upper Bollinger band distance (ρ=-0.50) and 15-day momentum (ρ=-0.42) confirm this. The deeper the hole, the stronger the snapback.

- **Bounce on SPY-green days, skip SPY-neutral days.** When SPY closes green on bounce day: 91% win rate, +11.44% avg P&L (N=67). When SPY is neutral (flat): 69% win rate, -3.99% avg P&L (N=13). That's a 15-point swing. The p-value is 0.052 — borderline significant but the effect size is enormous and the direction makes intuitive sense (capitulation bounces need a risk-on catalyst).

- **Premarket volume is a quality signal for reversals.** Higher premarket volume relative to total volume (ρ=0.48) predicts better reversal shorts. Conversely, when the 5-min regular session volume dwarfs premarket volume (vol_ratio_5min_to_pm, ρ=-0.48), the reversal underperforms. Translation: the best parabolic shorts happen when the hype starts in premarket, not when it ramps at the open.

- **Higher-ATR stocks bounce harder.** ATR% is the only positive-direction predictor in the bounce top-10 (ρ=0.38). Volatile names give you more to work with on the bounce. This aligns with the exit target framework already using ATR multiples.

### 2. KEY TAKEAWAYS

**For Reversal Shorts:**
The feature importance analysis across 112 trades (29 of 32 features significant!) paints a clear picture: **extension is everything.** The top 5 features are all flavors of "how far has this thing run?" — day-of range expansion, distance from moving averages, and multi-day momentum. This validates the existing scorer's emphasis on pct_from_9ema and range/ATR, but suggests day_of_range_pct deserves more weight than it currently gets.

The premarket volume signal is the most interesting *new* finding. It suggests there's a quality difference between stocks that gap up on heavy premarket volume (better shorts) vs. stocks that ramp after the open. The former may represent overnight hype that fades; the latter may represent real demand.

**For Bounce Longs:**
The 3-day selloff depth has a ρ of -0.68 — that's an unusually strong correlation for financial data. It means the pre-trade checklist criterion for "deep selloff" should be weighted heavily, and you should be sizing up on the deepest capitulations.

The regime finding is tactically useful: bounces performed massively better on days SPY closed green (+11.44%) vs. neutral (-3.99%). The SPY-trend analysis also shows bounces work best in SPY downtrends (N=65, +11.32%), which makes sense — that's when you get real capitulation setups. Combining these: **the ideal bounce setup is a deep capitulation during a broader SPY pullback, entered on a day when SPY catches a bid.**

### 3. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS

1. **Add a minimum extension filter for reversals.** Consider a hard floor on pct_from_9ema (e.g., top quartile only) or day_of_range_pct. The correlation data says low-extension reversal trades are destroying your average — they dilute expectancy even if they "technically" meet criteria. Run the threshold experiment to find the cut.

2. **Weight the bounce pre-trade checklist toward selloff depth.** pct_change_3 at ρ=-0.68 is nearly 2x the next-best predictor. The current checklist treats all 5 criteria roughly equally. Consider making "deep selloff" a gate criterion: if the selloff isn't deep enough, skip it regardless of other factors.

3. **Add a SPY-daily regime overlay to bounce trading.** On SPY-neutral days, the bounce expectancy is negative. Consider: (a) reducing size on SPY-flat days, (b) waiting for SPY to show direction before entering, or (c) skipping bounces entirely when SPY is range-bound. This needs validation with more data (N=13 for neutral is thin).

4. **Track premarket volume ratio for reversals.** Start logging percent_of_premarket_vol as a real-time signal in `reversal_trader.py` if not already surfaced. Prioritize setups where the stock has significant premarket volume — these are your highest-conviction shorts.
