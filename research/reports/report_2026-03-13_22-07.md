Weak stocks that have been beaten down for 3+ days (3DGapFade_weakstock) are where the real alpha is.

### Walk-Forward Stability (SIGNIFICANT)

**Bounce: STABLE.** The grading system holds up out-of-sample. OOS win rate averaged 90.3% across splits, and the latest split (mid-2024) showed 97% WR in validation. This is a durable edge.

**Reversal: MODERATELY STABLE — all 3 splits degraded.** The pattern is consistent: train period shows near-perfect results, then OOS drops to ~85% WR and ~8% avg P&L. This doesn't mean the strategy is broken — 85% WR and 8% avg is still very good — but it means the in-sample numbers of 93% WR and 13.4% avg are overstated. **Your realistic reversal expectancy is closer to +8%, not +13%.** Plan position sizing accordingly.

### Reversal Exit Optimization (Informative)

The trailing stop analysis reveals a clear structural inefficiency:

| Exit Method | Avg P&L | vs Hold-to-Close |
|---|---|---|
| 25% trailing from peak | +17.34% | **+3.84%** |
| 50% trailing from peak | +14.17% | +0.67% |
| Hold to close | +13.50% | baseline |

The 25% trailing stop outperforms by nearly $4 per trade. But there's a nuance by cap:
- **Large caps** barely move — only 14% hit 2x ATR. Take quick profits.
- **Small caps** run hard — 80% hit 2x ATR. Let them work with a trailing stop.
- **Medium caps** are in between but have the worst capture efficiency (11.1%), meaning they give back the most. These need the tightest management.

### SPY Regime (Bounce — Borderline Significant, p=0.052)

The mechanical logic holds: capitulation bounces need buyers to step in. When SPY is green, the broad market provides that buying pressure. When SPY is flat or red, the bounce attempt lacks fuel.

## 4. NEXT SESSION IDEAS

### 1. Reversal Cap-Stratified Exit Backtest (Highest Priority)
**Why:** This session quantified the exit gap (32.5% capture efficiency) and showed Large vs Small caps behave completely differently. But the trailing stop numbers are theoretical — they assume you can trail from the exact peak. A realistic backtest using intraday bar data to simulate a trailing stop (e.g., "trail 25% from highest close of any 5-min bar after entry") would tell you what's actually achievable. The potential improvement is +$3-4 per trade on 110+ trades.

### 2. Reversal Degradation Diagnosis
**Why:** All 3 walk-forward splits degraded. Is it because early trades (2022-2023) were in a different regime (high vol, bear market)? Or because the grading thresholds were optimized on that early data? Stratify the degradation by cap and setup type to find which bucket is causing the OOS decline. If it's one cap class degrading while others hold, cap-specific adjustments could fix it.

### 3. Bounce Exit Optimization
**Why:** We haven't run exit analysis on bounces yet. Given the bounce strategy's stability, exit improvements would compound on a reliable base. The bounce MFE/MAE data would tell us whether bounces also give back profit or whether the current hold-to-close is already near-optimal.

## 5. LESSONS LEARNED

- **Walk-forward testing is the most important experiment in the toolkit.** The reversal strategy looked bulletproof on paper (93% WR, +13.4% avg). Walk-forward revealed consistent degradation across every split. Without this test, we'd be trading with inflated expectations. Every strategy should be walk-forward tested before trusting the headline numbers.

- **Setup type comparison on bounces was the highest-value experiment this session.** One clear signal: avoid IntradayCapitch_strongstock (p=0.007). This is a concrete, actionable rule that doesn't require threshold tuning or filter optimization. Binary decisions (trade/don't trade this type) are more robust than continuous thresholds.

- **The filter sweep on bounce features produced nothing useful, confirming the prior lesson.** 56 single filters and 1 combination tested — best p-value was 0.158. With N=95 and feature-rich data, if there were a clean filter threshold that dramatically improved results, we'd have found it by now. Entry refinement for bounces is tapped out. The alpha is in setup selection (which types to trade) and exit management (when to take profit).

- **Regime analysis is converging on a reliable finding.** SPY green vs neutral for bounces has now been flagged across sessions, with the p-value tightening to 0.052. It's not yet statistically significant at the 0.05 level, but the N=67 green bucket is large, the effect is mechanically logical, and it's been consistent. This is the kind of finding worth acting on even without a clean p-value — the downside of being wrong (slightly suboptimal sizing on flat SPY days) is small.

- **In-sample vs out-of-sample gap is the real metric.** Reversal shows +13.4% in-sample but +8.1% OOS. Bounce shows +8.6% in-sample and +6.2% OOS. The bounce strategy's smaller gap (28% degradation vs 40% for reversals) makes it the more trustworthy edge. When planning capital allocation between the two strategies, weight bounce more heavily than the raw expectancy numbers suggest.
