### 2. KEY TAKEAWAYS

**Reversal Trailing Stop (Highest Impact)**

The average reversal trade reaches a peak unrealized gain of 21.97% (median 17.3%) but only captures 13.50% by close — giving back an average of 17.07 percentage points after peaking. A mechanical 25% trailing stop (lock in 75% of peak, let 25% ride) would have captured +17.34% avg. Even a 50% trailing stop captures +14.17%, still beating hold-to-close. This isn't a marginal improvement — it's a structural inefficiency in how profits are being managed.

The time-based analysis tells the same story: P&L builds steadily through 1pm (+11.54%) then flatlines or decays into close (+13.50%). The last two hours of holding add very little on average.

**Bounce SPY Regime (High Confidence)**

The ideal bounce setup: SPY has been weak (downtrend) AND SPY is green on the specific bounce day. This makes intuitive sense — you want to buy the capitulation bottom on the day the market turns. The numbers back it up strongly:
- SPY green day bounces: +11.44%, 91% WR, N=67
- SPY neutral day bounces: -3.99%, 69% WR, N=13
- SPY down-trend context: +11.32%, 89% WR, N=65

The SPY neutral day finding (negative expectancy) is concerning but N=13 is borderline. Worth watching but not worth making a hard rule yet.

**Reversal ATR Exits by Cap**

Large caps exhaust quickly — only 14.3% reach 2x ATR. Medium and Small caps have much more runway. This means:
- Large/ETF: Take 1x ATR and be happy (80-86% hit rate)
- Medium: Reasonable to target 1.5x ATR (64.5% hit rate)
- Small: Can target 2x ATR (80% hit rate) — these are where the big moves live

### 5. LESSONS LEARNED

- **Exit analysis is where the money is — confirmed.** Prior sessions kept trying to refine entries and found nothing. This session quantified the exit gap: 32.5% capture efficiency means two-thirds of potential profit is being left on the table or given back. Entry optimization has diminishing returns when WR is already 84-93%. Exit optimization is a wide-open field.

- **"Not significant" doesn't mean "not useful."** All three experiments were flagged as not significant, but the exit optimization data is extremely actionable. The trailing stop finding (+3.84% improvement) doesn't need a p-value — it's a structural observation about how much profit is given back, not a fragile filter threshold.

- **Regime analysis works best when it tells a coherent story.** The SPY findings aren't just statistically interesting — they make mechanical sense. Capitulation bounces should work best when the market has been weak (creating the capitulation) and then turns green (providing the catalyst). When findings align with market logic, they're more likely to hold out-of-sample.

- **Repeated null results on entry features should be treated as a definitive answer.** Two sessions now have found zero significant entry predictors for reversals across 30+ features. This is not a failure of methodology — it's a real finding. The grading system works. Stop running feature importance on reversals. The alpha is in exits and sizing.

- **Cap stratification of exits is more important than cap stratification of entries.** Large caps hitting 2x ATR only 14% of the time vs Small caps at 80% is a massive difference in how you should manage the trade. A one-size-fits-all exit target is leaving money on the table for Small caps and creating false expectations for Large caps.
